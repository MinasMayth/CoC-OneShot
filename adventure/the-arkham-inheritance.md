## 1. Keeper's Background

> *This section is for the Keeper only. Do not share with players.*

### Full History

**Professor Edmund Whitmore** (68) spent forty years at Miskatonic University teaching ancient history and comparative religion. He was brilliant, obsessive, and increasingly reclusive in his final decade. Three months ago, he returned from a solo expedition to the rural village of **Dunham's Crossing**, 40 miles northwest of Arkham, where he had been investigating reports of a strange cult — *The Brotherhood of the Crimson Sign* — whose members had vanished abruptly in 1917.

In the ruins of the cult's meeting-house, Whitmore discovered a carved stone roughly the size and shape of a brick, covered in glyphs that predated any known human script. His journals describe it as *"warm to the touch despite the cold, and somehow heavier than its size should allow."* He brought it back to his Arkham townhouse for study.

The stone is a **Ghoul Summoning Focus**, an artifact of unknown origin used by the Brotherhood to commune with ghouls — corpse-eating creatures that dwell in vast tunnel networks beneath human graveyards and cities. Within two weeks of bringing the stone home, Whitmore heard scratching beneath his cellar floor. He reinforced the trapdoor with iron bolts and continued his research, apparently believing he could learn to communicate with whatever he had attracted.

He was wrong. On the night of October 11th, **the ghoul broke through**. The housekeeper, **Mrs Agnes Farrow**, discovered Whitmore's body the following morning — mauled, partially consumed, and with an expression of absolute terror. She fled and has not returned. The police ruled it a "wild animal attack" and closed the case (Arkham's law enforcement prefers simple explanations). The solicitor Arthur Holt, unaware of the supernatural details, has been trying to contact the named beneficiaries.

**The ghoul is still in the basement.** It cannot easily leave — it entered through a tunnel that connects to the graveyard three blocks away, but the stone's resonance keeps drawing it back. It is now territorial and aggressive. The stone must be destroyed or removed (ideally buried far from any graveyard) to break the connection.

### The Secret the Players Can Uncover

The professor's final research notes (found in a locked drawer in his study) reveal:
- What the stone is and does
- That destroying the stone with fire does not work (he tried a fragment)
- That *burying it in running water* (a riverbed) or *shattering it with a blessed implement* (there is a silver letter-opener blessed by a priest in his desk) should sever the connection
- A warning: *"The creature has my scent now. Burning the stone alone will not satisfy it."*

The ghoul can be driven off permanently only if **both conditions are met**: the stone is neutralised AND the ghoul is driven back into its tunnel. It does not need to be killed.

## 2. Quick Rules Reference for the Keeper

### Skill Rolls
- Roll **d100** (two ten-sided dice, one for tens, one for units; 00+0 = 100)
- **Success:** Roll ≤ skill value
- **Hard Success:** Roll ≤ skill/2 (round down) — required when the task is genuinely difficult
- **Extreme Success:** Roll ≤ skill/5 (round down) — exceptional outcome
- **Failure:** Roll > skill value
- **Fumble:** Roll 96–100 (or 100 if skill > 50) — catastrophic failure

### When to Call for a Roll
Only call for a roll when:
1. There is a meaningful chance of failure, AND
2. Failure has interesting consequences

If a character has a skill at 70%+ and the task is routine, just let them succeed. Reserve rolls for tense, important moments.

### Combat Summary
1. **Determine initiative:** Highest DEX goes first. Ties broken by higher DEX value.
2. **On your turn:** Make one action (attack, dodge, use item, flee, first aid on yourself, etc.)
3. **Attacking:** Roll ≤ Fighting (Brawl) or Firearms skill
4. **Defending:** Either **Dodge** (roll ≤ Dodge, uses your action) or **Fight Back** (both sides roll attack; lower success level wins)
5. **Damage:** Roll the weapon's damage dice and subtract from target's HP
6. **Major Wound:** Losing ≥ half max HP in one hit requires a CON roll or fall unconscious
7. **Dying:** 0 HP = dying; -2 HP = dead

### Sanity Rolls
When investigators encounter something horrific, call for a **Sanity roll (d100 ≤ current SAN)**:
- **Success:** Lose the minimum listed SAN
- **Failure:** Lose the maximum listed SAN

**Temporary Insanity:** Losing 5+ SAN in one roll triggers a Temporary Insanity check. The character may freeze, flee, laugh uncontrollably, or suffer a short-lived delusion. Roll on the **Temporary Insanity Table** below or choose something thematically appropriate.

**Indefinite Insanity:** Losing 20+ SAN in a single session triggers Indefinite Insanity.

#### Temporary Insanity Table (d10)
| d10 | Result |
|---|---|
| 1 | Freezes in place; cannot act for 1d3 rounds |
| 2 | Flees the area as fast as possible for 1d6 rounds |
| 3 | Violent outburst — attacks nearest creature (including allies) for 1 round |
| 4 | Faints; recovers in 1d6 minutes |
| 5 | Hysterical laughter; cannot take meaningful action for 1d3 rounds |
| 6 | Screams uncontrollably for 1d3 rounds; others lose 1 SAN hearing it |
| 7 | Crawls into corner and rocks, muttering; 1d3 rounds |
| 8 | Denial — insists nothing supernatural is happening; 1 scene |
| 9 | Paranoia — convinced one ally is behind everything; 1 scene |
| 10 | Phantom sensation — believes something is crawling on their skin; distracted (−20% to all skills) for 1d6 rounds |

## 3. The Hook

### Reading the Letter Aloud to Players

> *Each investigator has received the following letter by post, dated October 12, 1923:*

---

*Dear [investigator name],*

*I write to you on behalf of my client, the late Professor Edmund Whitmore, who passed from this world on the evening of October 11th. The circumstances were most unfortunate — a tragic accident — and the city mourns the loss of one of Miskatonic University's most distinguished faculty.*

*Professor Whitmore named you as a beneficiary in his last will and testament. I request your attendance at my offices at 14 Pickman Street, Arkham, at two o'clock in the afternoon on Monday, October 14th, at which time the will shall be read in full.*

*You will find the matter to your considerable advantage.*

*Yours faithfully,*  
*Arthur Holt, Esquire*  
*Holt & Associates, Solicitors*

---

**Keeper's Note:** Each player should decide *how* their character knew Professor Whitmore. Suggestions by character:
- **Frank Malone (P.I.):** Whitmore hired Frank to track down a student who'd stolen research materials. They developed a mutual respect.
- **Dr. Helena Cross (Doctor):** Helena was Whitmore's physician; he often consulted her about "unusual physical complaints."
- **Samuel Okafor (Journalist):** Sam interviewed Whitmore twice for the Arkham Advertiser; Whitmore trusted him to "tell the truth."
- **Agatha Pemberton (Dilettante):** Agatha and Whitmore were old family friends; she funded some of his expeditions.
- **Dr. James Fairweather (Professor):** Whitmore was James's mentor and department head at Miskatonic.

## 4. Scene 1 — The Reading of the Will

**Location:** Office of Arthur Holt, Solicitor, 14 Pickman Street  
**Time:** 2:00 PM, Monday, October 14th

### Description (read or paraphrase to players)

> *The office of Arthur Holt is what you expected: dark wood panelling, bookshelves crammed with legal volumes, the smell of pipe tobacco and old paper. A brass clock ticks on the mantelpiece. Holt himself is a trim, nervous man in his fifties — he keeps straightening the papers on his desk as if they keep moving on their own.*
>
> *You are shown into the meeting room. The other beneficiaries are already seated — [introduce the other investigators to each other if needed]. A chair in the corner is conspicuously empty: a placard reads "Mrs Agnes Farrow." Holt clears his throat.*
>
> *"Thank you all for coming. I'll dispense with the usual pleasantries and proceed directly to the matter at hand."*

### The Will's Contents

Arthur Holt reads the following:

1. **To all named beneficiaries jointly:** The deed to 7 Crane Street, Arkham (Whitmore's townhouse), to be maintained, sold, or otherwise disposed of by unanimous decision.
2. **To Frank Malone:** Whitmore's Webley revolver and a box of ammunition. *"He will know what to do with it."*
3. **To Dr. Helena Cross:** Whitmore's personal medical bag and his collection of pharmacological texts.
4. **To Samuel Okafor:** Whitmore's typewriter and his complete personal journals, *"with the instruction that he publish nothing until he understands everything."*
5. **To Agatha Pemberton:** Whitmore's full financial estate (£3,200) and his private art collection.
6. **To Dr. James Fairweather:** Whitmore's personal library and all research materials, *"to continue what I could not finish."*
7. **To all beneficiaries jointly:** *"I ask — I beg — that you go to the house together. Do not go alone. Look in the study. Find what I found. You will understand. Destroy it. I am sorry."*

**The mysterious final clause** (read slowly and dramatically) immediately unsettles the room.

### What Arthur Holt Knows

Holt is cooperative but clearly shaken. He will share the following if asked:

- Mrs Agnes Farrow (the housekeeper) was first to find the body. She called the police, gave a brief statement, and then disappeared. He has been unable to reach her.
- The police ruled it a "wild animal" attack. Holt finds this implausible for an urban townhouse but is not inclined to press the matter.
- Whitmore had become "increasingly eccentric" in recent months, referring to something he brought back from an expedition as *"a stone that listens."*
- He has the key to 7 Crane Street and will provide it. He does not want to go there himself.

### Skill Opportunities (Scene 1)

| Skill | Difficulty | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Psychology** (Holt) | Regular | Holt is genuinely frightened; he knows more than he's saying but isn't lying outright |
| **Persuade** (Holt) | Regular | He reveals that the police photos showed claw marks *inside* a locked room — impossible for any normal animal |
| **Spot Hidden** | Regular | A sealed envelope addressed to *"Whoever Reads This Last"* is tucked under Holt's blotter — see **Handout A** |
| **Law** | Regular | The investigators are now legally responsible for the property and its contents |

## 5. Scene 2 — Whitmore Townhouse: Ground Floor

**Location:** 7 Crane Street, Arkham  
**Time:** Late afternoon, approaching dusk

### Exterior Description

> *Number 7 Crane Street is a narrow three-storey townhouse squeezed between a laundry and a closed pharmacy. The brickwork is old — Federal-era, you'd guess — and the upper windows are dark. A dead potted fern sits on the front step. The lock is stiff but yields to the solicitor's key.*

### Ground Floor Layout

```
[FRONT DOOR]
     |
[ENTRY HALL] — coatrack, umbrella stand, faded wallpaper with a water stain
     |
  [PARLOUR] ←→ [DINING ROOM]
     |
[KITCHEN] — door to backyard
     |
[CELLAR DOOR] — at the end of the kitchen passage
```

### Room Descriptions

#### Entry Hall
- A telephone on a small table (works; police can be called but will not help)
- A calendar on the wall — October 11th is circled in red ink with the note: *"IT COMES TONIGHT"*
- Three deep parallel claw marks in the lower third of the kitchen passage wall (waist height for a human; ankle height for something much taller)

> **Spot Hidden (Regular):** The claw marks are not animal — each mark is 4 inches apart, consistent with a hand roughly double human size.

#### Parlour
- Dusty, unused-looking; the furniture is covered with white sheets
- One sheet has been pulled off the armchair and left in a heap — something sat here recently
- On the floor beside the armchair: a used candle, a piece of chalk forming a partial circle, and three drops of dried blood (not from any wound; part of a ritual)

> **Occult (Regular):** The partial chalk circle is a *ward* of some kind — Whitmore was trying to protect himself. It was never completed.

#### Dining Room
- A half-eaten meal on the table, three days old and moulding (Whitmore's last supper)
- A newspaper: *Arkham Advertiser, October 11* — turned to the obituary page, with Whitmore's name circled in pencil. **History/Library Use (Hard):** This is actually an older paper whose date has been altered in pen — Whitmore circled his *own* death notice after finding it among old issues during his research into the cult's victims. A deliberate, disturbing reminder of his own mortality once he realised the danger he was in.
- A locked wooden box under the table (**STR roll or Locksmith to open**): contains a .38 revolver with four rounds loaded and a note — *"For emergencies."* (If Frank Malone is present, he recognises this as the weapon Whitmore used to hire him with; Whitmore always kept it under the table.)

#### Kitchen
- Normal kitchen. Pantry still stocked.
- **Spot Hidden (Regular):** Fresh mud on the kitchen floor leading *from* the cellar door, not toward it. Something came up, not down.
- The cellar door has three slide bolts — all currently *retracted*. Deep scratch marks on the door frame on the kitchen side.

### Tension Note for the Keeper

As the investigators search the ground floor, have them make a **Listen roll (Regular)**. Success: they hear a faint rhythmic scratching from below — not constant, but intermittent, like something shifting its weight. This is the ghoul in the basement. It is awake and aware the house has visitors. Do not reveal what it is yet; let the sound fester.

## 6. Scene 3 — The Study & Clues

**Location:** Second floor, 7 Crane Street  
**Time:** Late afternoon into evening

### Reaching the Study

The stairs creak loudly (unavoidable). There are three rooms on the second floor: a bathroom, a guest bedroom, and the **study** at the rear of the house.

### Study Description

> *The study is the most lived-in room in the house — every surface is covered in books, journals, loose papers, and archaeological sketches. A large oak desk faces the window overlooking a narrow alley. The gaslight is still set to a low flame; someone clearly spent their last hours in this room.*
>
> *The bookshelves lining the walls contain texts in at least six languages. A large map of rural Massachusetts is pinned to one wall, with a location circled in red ink 40 miles northwest. The word "DUNHAM'S CROSSING" is written in a shaking hand.*
>
> *Most significantly: the bottom right desk drawer is open and empty. Something has been removed.*

### Clues in the Study

#### Clue 1 — The Research Journals (on the desk)
Samuel Okafor's inheritance. Five leather-bound journals spanning forty years. The most recent (Journal V) describes the Dunham's Crossing expedition.

**Key passages (Handout B):**

> *"...the Brotherhood of the Crimson Sign vanished in 1917 without explanation. The villagers of Dunham's Crossing will not speak of them. I found the meeting-house myself, three miles into the woods. The Circle is still there. The stone was in the centre."*

> *"...I know what it is now. It is a Focus — an attractant, like a hunting call fashioned in stone. The Brotherhood used it to commune with the creatures that dwell below. I have heard them in my cellar for eleven days."*

> *"...I have reinforced the trapdoor. It will not hold forever. The creatures — it, there is only one, I believe — is persistent. It wants the stone back. Or perhaps it simply wants me."*

#### Clue 2 — The Locked Drawer (right-hand middle drawer)
Requires **Locksmith (Regular)** or **STR (Hard)** to open.

Contains: the professor's **final notes** (Handout C) and a **silver letter-opener** (approximately 8 inches, engraved with a cross and the words *"St Brendan's Parish, 1901"* — a gift from a priest friend).

**Final Notes (Handout C) — crucial information:**

> *"The stone cannot be destroyed by fire or ordinary force — I chipped a fragment off and attempted to burn it. The fragment regenerated overnight. The Focus must be either:*
> *(a) Submerged in running water (a riverbed — the Miskatonic runs two blocks east), or*
> *(b) Struck with a blessed implement*
>
> *I have the silver letter-opener. I should have done this weeks ago.*
>
> *Warning: neutralising the stone will not immediately drive the creature away. It will be confused and frightened but still dangerous. It must be shown the way back to its tunnel. It understands spoken commands — they have a language. I have written the command below.*
>
> *The command to depart: say clearly and firmly — YOTH'KARA MNASHOL — three times. Do not show fear. They despise weakness but respond to authority.*
>
> *God help whoever reads this. God help me."*

#### Clue 3 — The Map (on the wall)
The circled location is Dunham's Crossing. A route is marked. A sticky note reads: *"The tunnel system — they travel beneath the graveyard. St Anselm's is connected to this house via the old colonial drainage network."*

> **Spot Hidden (Hard):** A small diagram in the margin shows the basement layout with a marked trapdoor and the location of the stone.

#### Clue 4 — Occult Books
**Library Use (Regular):** The shelf contains a copy of *"Nameless Cults"* (a German text) and a hand-annotated section of the *Arkham Folklore Compendium*. The annotations describe ghouls:
- They are ancient, pre-human beings
- They dwell in tunnel systems beneath graveyards
- They communicate in a guttural language combining barks, whines, and clicks
- They are not mindlessly violent — they retreat from authority and fire
- Silver burns them (minor detail, relevant only if the letter-opener is used as a weapon)

#### Clue 5 — The Newspaper Clipping (pinned to the wall)
*Arkham Advertiser, September 4, 1923:* **"Miskatonic Groundskeepers Report Strange Smells Near East Wing Basement."** The university's east wing shares the same colonial drainage tunnel network. This is background flavour — it confirms the tunnel network is real and active.

### Skill Opportunities (Scene 2 & 3 Combined)

| Skill | Difficulty | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Spot Hidden** | Regular | Find the map margin diagram; find the chalk circle remnants downstairs |
| **Occult** | Regular | Recognise the chalk ward; understand the general nature of the stone |
| **Library Use** | Regular | Find the ghoul references in Whitmore's annotated books |
| **Psychology** | Regular | Reading the journals, the investigators sense Whitmore was genuinely terrified but also fascinated — he did not want to destroy the stone until the last moment |
| **History/Archaeology** | Hard | The script on the stone (if seen) is not of any known ancient human civilisation |
| **Medicine** | Regular | Examining the claw-mark photos in Whitmore's notes confirms the marks are not from any animal catalogued by science |

## 7. Scene 4 — The Basement

**Location:** Cellar, 7 Crane Street  
**Time:** Evening (the investigators should ideally go down after finding at least Clue 2 and preferably Clue 3)

### The Descent

> *The cellar door opens onto steep wooden stairs descending into darkness. The smell hits you immediately — damp earth, old meat, something musky and animal. The single light bulb at the bottom flickers. Someone has twisted it half-out of its socket.*

If the investigators brought a lamp or torch: they can see clearly. If not, they are in near-darkness (−20% to all rolls requiring sight).

### Cellar Layout

```
[STAIRS]
    |
[MAIN CELLAR AREA] — coal bin (right), wine rack (left, mostly empty)
    |
[BACK AREA] — workbench, shelving, iron-bolted trapdoor in the floor
                                    [THE STONE] — on the workbench
```

### The Stone

> *On the workbench at the far end of the cellar sits an object roughly the size and shape of a brick. It is dark grey, almost black, carved with interlocking geometric symbols that seem to shift when you look at them sideways. It radiates a faint warmth despite the cold cellar air. Something about it makes you deeply uneasy.*

**Sanity Roll (0/1d3):** Seeing the stone for the first time. On a failure, the investigator feels a compulsion to pick it up; they may resist with a **POW×5 roll**.

**Occult (Regular):** This is definitely a ritual artefact of significant power. The symbols pre-date known human civilisations.  
**Occult (Hard):** The warmth is not thermal — it is a form of psychic resonance, calling to something below.

### The Trapdoor

The bolts have been forced from below — three iron bolts have been bent and torn out of the wood. The trapdoor lies slightly ajar. A gust of cold tunnel air rises from the gap.

**Listen (Regular):** The scratching sound from upstairs is now clearly audible and much closer — just below the trapdoor.

### The Ghoul Emerges

**When the investigators approach the trapdoor or pick up the stone, it erupts open.**

> *The trapdoor flies open with a crash that shakes the shelving. What pulls itself up into your lantern light is like nothing in the natural world. It is roughly humanoid — six feet tall but hunched, with elongated limbs and fingers that bend the wrong direction. Its skin is grey-white and hairless, its eyes a deep amber that reflects light like a cat's. Its mouth is too wide, filled with teeth like a dog's, and it wears the rags of clothing on its lower body — ancient, rotted cloth. It turns its head toward you slowly and opens its mouth in a sound between a whine and a bark.*

**Sanity Roll (1/1d6):** Seeing the ghoul for the first time. Characters who fail may suffer Temporary Insanity (see table in Section 2).

## 8. Scene 5 — Confrontation & Resolution

### The Ghoul's Behaviour

The ghoul is **not immediately attacking**. It is territorial, confused, and attracted to the stone. It will:

1. **First round:** Stare at the investigators, sniffing the air. It does not charge immediately.
2. **Second round:** If investigators take no action, it moves toward the stone. If someone stands between it and the stone, it growls and postures (a warning).
3. **Third round onward:** If it cannot reach the stone (someone is holding it or it's been moved), the ghoul attacks.
4. **If attacked first:** It immediately fights back.

### Resolution Options

There are three ways to end this encounter. Encourage players to find creative solutions — the adventure rewards players who used the clues.

#### Option A — Use the Silver Letter-Opener on the Stone (Best Outcome)
> *If an investigator strikes the stone with the silver letter-opener:*

The stone cracks with a sound like breaking bone. A wave of psychic pressure rolls through the basement (**Sanity Roll 0/1** for all present). The warmth drains out of it instantly. The fragments are now inert.

The ghoul **freezes**, then makes a long, low keening sound. It looks around the basement with apparent confusion, then moves back toward the trapdoor. If an investigator then speaks the command (*YOTH'KARA MNASHOL*, three times, firm voice), the ghoul drops into the tunnel and the trapdoor can be closed.

> **POW×5 roll to speak the command without flinching.** On a failure, the character hesitates; another investigator must try.

**Outcome:** Ghoul retreats. Stone destroyed. Threat ended. No one needs to die.

#### Option B — Throw the Stone in the Miskatonic River (Slower, More Dangerous)
Someone must carry the stone (attractive to the ghoul — it will follow) two blocks east to the Miskatonic River and submerge it. The ghoul will pursue through the streets.

- This is more dramatic but more dangerous (see Running a Street Chase below)
- Once the stone is submerged, the ghoul stops, circles, and eventually retreats underground

**Outcome:** Same end result as Option A. Chaotic, good for players who want action.

#### Option C — Drive the Ghoul Back With Fire and Flee
Investigators who use torches, lanterns, or Molotov cocktails (petrol from the kitchen, a rag, a match) can drive the ghoul back through the trapdoor temporarily. This does not solve the problem permanently — the ghoul will return — but it buys time.

If the investigators flee without dealing with the stone:
- The threat is not ended (the Keeper can note this at the end)
- The investigators still have the stone and must deal with it later
- This is not a "failure" — surviving is a valid CoC success

#### Option D — Kill the Ghoul (Hard, Dangerous)
Statistics below. A ghoul with 16 HP and a bite that deals 1d4+2d6 damage is *extremely dangerous* to ordinary humans. This is not recommended, but players can try. If reduced to 0 HP, the ghoul collapses and eventually dissolves, leaving only its rags — ghouls cannot exist above ground for long after death.

### Running a Street Chase (if Option B)
Use simplified chase rules:
1. Each round, both ghoul and fleeing investigator roll **DEX×5**
2. If the investigator succeeds and the ghoul fails: gains ground
3. If the ghoul succeeds and the investigator fails: ghoul gains ground (attack next round if within range)
4. Both succeed/fail: no change
5. Reach the river in 5 rounds of successful ground-gaining

### After the Confrontation

Once the threat is neutralised:

> *The basement is silent. Whatever was there — whether because you drove it back, destroyed its anchor, or simply outlasted it — is gone. The trapdoor gapes open into darkness. Somewhere far below, you hear faint shuffling, retreating, growing fainter.*
>
> *You are still alive. That, in this city, on this night, is something.*

**Award 1d6 SAN to each surviving investigator** (the reward for successfully resolving a Mythos threat without anyone dying, per CoC 7e rules).

If someone died: 1d4 SAN to survivors.  
If the stone was destroyed: additional +1 SAN.  
If the command was used successfully: additional +1 SAN (they communicated with something inhuman and won).

## 9. Endings

### Full Success
The stone is destroyed, the ghoul returns to its tunnels, and all investigators are alive. They may report to Arthur Holt, who pays them their inheritance and quietly buries the incident. They inherit the townhouse. Closing image: they stand on the front step of 7 Crane Street in the cold October dawn. The city is waking up around them. They know something most people don't — and that knowledge has a cost.

### Partial Success
The threat is reduced but not fully resolved (e.g. stone submerged but not destroyed; ghoul driven off but not gone). The investigators live. Things are better but not fixed. Suitable for a sequel.

### Failure (But Surviving)
The investigators flee without dealing with the stone. The house remains dangerous. They inherited it and are now legally responsible for whatever happens there. A dark ending — responsible investigators will have to come back.

### Total Party Kill
If all investigators die or are incapacitated — the ghoul eventually leaves the house once the stone fades over time (weeks). The mystery goes unsolved. Holt eventually sells the property. The new residents... do not last long.

**Personality:** Professional, slightly cowardly, genuinely fond of Whitmore. He knows more than he says but isn't hiding anything malicious — he's just frightened and prefers not to think about it.

**Statistics (if needed for social rolls):**  
STR 40 · CON 50 · SIZ 55 · DEX 45 · APP 60 · INT 65 · POW 55 · EDU 75  
HP 10 · MP 11 · SAN 50 · Build 0 · Damage Bonus None · Move 7

**Skills:** Law 80%, Persuade 60%, Fast Talk 50%, Psychology 40%, Accounting 70%

**What He'll Share Freely:** The contents of the will, the police report, his inability to reach Mrs Farrow.  
**What He'll Share If Persuaded:** The police photos showed claw marks inside a locked room. He does not believe it was an animal.  
**What He Won't Share (doesn't know):** Anything about the stone or the cult.

**Role:** Potential witness (she found the body); she has fled to her sister's house in Dunwich  
**Description:** Stout woman in her 60s; Scottish accent; deeply religious; very shaken.

## 11. The Ghoul — Statistics & Tactics

### Background

Ghouls are ancient, pre-human entities that feed on corpses and dwell in vast underground tunnel networks beneath human graveyards and cities. They are not supernatural in the Mythos sense — they are a form of life that evolved alongside humanity but beneath it. They are intelligent but alien in their motivations. They understand human speech (and can learn it, over time) but rarely speak it. They communicate among themselves in a language of barks, whines, and clicks.

This specific ghoul was drawn to Whitmore's house by the summoning stone. It is territorial, hungry, and confused by the stone's continuing resonance. It is not evil in the moral sense — it is an animal defending what it considers its territory.

### Special Abilities

**Darkvision:** The ghoul can see perfectly in complete darkness. Light sources do not give investigators any advantage in darkness that the ghoul doesn't also negate.

**Tunnel Sense:** The ghoul always knows the location of the nearest tunnel entrance and will move toward it when severely wounded or frightened.

**Silver Vulnerability:** Weapons made of or coated in silver deal an additional 1d6 damage to ghouls. The silver letter-opener deals 1d6+db as a weapon.

**Resilience:** Ordinary bullets deal half damage (round up). The ghoul's grey skin is unnaturally tough. Firearms are less effective than melee weapons with silver.

**Fear Aura:** On first encountering the ghoul, all investigators make a Sanity roll (1/1d6). On a fumbled Sanity roll (roll of 96–100), the character is Temporarily Insane.

### Wound Thresholds

| HP | Status |
|---|---|
| 15–9 | Healthy; full speed, full aggression |
| 8–4 | Wounded; −10% to all attack rolls; may hesitate one round |
| 3–1 | Critically wounded; attempts to retreat to tunnel |
| 0 | Unconscious/dying; body begins to dissolve over 1d6 hours |

---

### Tactical Notes for the Keeper

- **The ghoul is not suicidal.** If it takes a Major Wound (loses ≥8 HP in one hit), it will attempt to retreat to the trapdoor. If the trapdoor is blocked, it will claw through walls — slowly.
- **Fire works.** Open flame does not deal extra damage but causes a Fear response: the ghoul must make a POW roll (50%) or retreat from any character holding a lit torch or lantern. Use this to let clever players without combat skills contribute.
- **The command words work.** If an investigator speaks *YOTH'KARA MNASHOL* three times in a firm voice (POW×5 roll), the ghoul pauses, whines, and begins backing toward the trapdoor. A second failure and it shakes off the effect and resumes aggression.
- **Do not use the ghoul as a pure combat encounter.** Let players try creative solutions. If they've read the professor's notes, they have everything they need. Reward players who use information over brute force.

---

### What Happens If The Ghoul Kills an Investigator

If an investigator is killed, the ghoul drags them toward the trapdoor. Other investigators who witness this make an additional **Sanity Roll (1d4/1d10)**. If not stopped (the ghoul will delay to feed), it takes the body into the tunnels. This is appropriately horrific.

## 13. Keeper Tips & Troubleshooting

### Pacing

- **Act 1 (The Hook & Will Reading):** 20–30 minutes. Establish relationships, build mystery, distribute information.
- **Act 2 (Ground Floor Investigation):** 20–30 minutes. Build dread. The scratching sound is your best tool.
- **Act 3 (The Study):** 30–40 minutes. This is the information dump — reward careful players. Make sure they find at least Handout C before the basement.
- **Act 4 (The Basement & Confrontation):** 30–60 minutes depending on approach.
- **Resolution:** 10–15 minutes.

### If Players Skip the Study

If players go directly to the basement without reading the notes, they will face the ghoul without the solution. This is **not a failure state** — it is a harder problem. The ghoul will chase them upstairs, which forces them to search faster. The notes are still available.

**Never lock players out of information permanently.** If they struggle, have them discover a clue they missed, or let a successful Occult or Library Use roll tell them what they need.

### If Players Want to Call the Police

They can. Officer Greer or a colleague will arrive in 20 minutes, inspect the claw marks, and tell them it was a wild animal. They will not go into the basement. They will leave. This is darkly funny and appropriate.

### If Players Are Too Cautious to Go Downstairs

Apply pressure: the scratching gets louder. Something falls over in the kitchen. A groan comes from below. If they still won't go down, the ghoul eventually pushes the cellar door open and comes up. This forces the confrontation.

### If Combat Goes Badly

CoC combat can turn lethal quickly. If an investigator is going to die from a single ghoul bite and it would feel unfair (e.g. the player forgot they had the silver letter-opener), allow a **Luck roll** to intervene — the ghoul is distracted by another character, or it fumbles its attack. One "save" per player is reasonable. After that, consequences should stand.

### Running the Sanity System

Sanity is one of CoC's most distinctive elements. Play it up:
- Before Sanity rolls, describe what the character sees/hears/smells vividly
- After a failed roll, describe the character's reaction physically (shaking hands, dry mouth, tunnel vision)
- Temporary insanity is not the end of the world — it's a story moment. Play it for drama, not punishment

### For Players New to CoC

Tell them upfront:
1. *"Your characters are ordinary people. They are not heroes. Combat is dangerous."*
2. *"Information is your most powerful tool. Read everything you find."*
3. *"Fleeing is a valid option. Surviving is a success."*
4. *"Your character's Sanity matters. It will go down. That's normal and part of the story."*

### Setting the Atmosphere

Music suggestions: Any ambient horror soundtrack (Brian Eno's *Music for Films*, Lustmord, or search "Cthulhu RPG ambient music" on YouTube). Keep lighting low if playing in person. Describe smells (the damp basement, the rotting food on the dining table, the musky animal scent). Speak slowly and quietly during horror reveals.
