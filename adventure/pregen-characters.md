# Pre-Generated Investigator Characters
### *The Arkham Inheritance* — Call of Cthulhu, 7th Edition

---

**How to Use These Sheets**

Print one per player. Let players choose which character interests them most. Each character has a built-in connection to Professor Whitmore (see **Background** for each). Skills are listed as percentages — roll d100 equal to or under to succeed.

> **Reminder for new players:**  
> Regular success = roll ≤ skill  
> Hard success = roll ≤ skill ÷ 2  
> Extreme success = roll ≤ skill ÷ 5  

---

## Character 1 — Frank Malone, Private Investigator

*"I've seen some things in this city that the papers don't print. I'm starting to think Whitmore was right to be scared."*

**Archetype:** Action investigator — best for players who want to *do things* and aren't afraid of a fight

---

| **Birthplace** | Boston, MA |
| **Personal Description** | Broad-shouldered, permanently creased suit, a jaw that's been broken twice and healed slightly wrong. Reads people well. Doesn't startle easily — or used to not. |

**Background:** Three months ago, Professor Whitmore hired Frank to recover stolen research materials from a light-fingered graduate student. ## Notes for the Keeper on Character Balance

- **Frank** is the only character built for combat; if players choose not to take him, combat will be more dangerous (as it should be in CoC)
- **Helena** provides healing — she can save the party in a tight spot; if players skip her, First Aid falls to whoever has the highest base skill (30%)
- **Sam** has the highest social skills and inherits the crucial journals; if another player takes him, make sure someone else picks up the journals in the will reading
- **Agatha** has the most resources (rifle, car, money) and the highest SAN — useful if a player is nervous about the SAN system; also has the only long-range combat option
- **James** is the best researcher; if players skip him, they may miss subtle clues — compensate by making those clues slightly more obvious

**The adventure works with any combination of 3–5 of these characters.** For a group of 3, consider giving each character one additional skill point to one relevant skill.
