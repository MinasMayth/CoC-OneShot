# The Arkham Inheritance — A Call of Cthulhu Oneshot

**System:** Call of Cthulhu, 7th Edition  
**Setting:** Arkham, Massachusetts, October 1923  
**Duration:** 3–5 hours  
**Players:** 3–5 (pre-generated characters included)  
**Difficulty:** Beginner-friendly — written for first-time players *and* Keepers (DMs)

## Adventure Summary

The investigators have been named as unexpected beneficiaries in the last will and testament of **Professor Edmund Whitmore**, an eccentric Miskatonic University antiquarian who died alone in his Arkham townhouse three days ago. They gather at the estate of solicitor **Arthur Holt** for the reading of the will — and discover they've each inherited a strange object, a share of Whitmore's wealth, and the deed to his townhouse.

When they visit the townhouse, however, they find signs of terror: claw marks in the study, a hidden trapdoor, and the professor's final research notes describing a **ghoul summoning stone** he recovered from a cult called *The Brotherhood of the Crimson Sign*. The stone is still in the basement. And so is what it called.

## Quick-Start for the Keeper

1. **Read the adventure once end-to-end** before running it (30–45 minutes).
2. **Print the character sheets** from `pregen-characters.md` — one per player. Let players pick the character that sounds most interesting to them.
3. **Print or prepare the handouts** listed at the end of `the-arkham-inheritance.md`.
4. **Gather:** 2 sets of dice (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, d100/percentile), pencils, scratch paper.
5. **Tone:** Emphasise dread and mystery over action. CoC is *not* D&D — combat is dangerous and fleeing is always a valid option.

## CoC in One Paragraph (for New Players)

Call of Cthulhu is a horror roleplaying game where you play ordinary people — professors, journalists, doctors — who uncover terrible secrets about the universe. Skills are rated 1–100; roll *equal to or under* your skill to succeed. Sanity is a resource that erodes as you encounter the supernatural. The goal is usually to *survive* and *stop the threat*, not to kill everything. Death and madness are real possibilities.
